About Me

I'm drawn to the places where quantum mechanics, computation, and materials science collide — and convinced the most interesting science lives at those intersections.

I'm an undergraduate at Colorado College double-majoring in Physics and Chemistry, where I also serve as president of the SPS chapter and compete as a student-athlete. My research spans quantum algorithm design, condensed matter simulation, and computational electrochemistry.

My interest in research grew through hands-on lab courses — cryogenic semiconductor transport, Michelson interferometry, He-Ne laser mode analysis — but my deepest work has been computational. I build simulation tools that treat quantum systems rigorously and at scale.

Through Northwestern University's MICRO Program and MRSEC REU under Dr. Roberto dos Reis, I developed QuScope — a quantum algorithm framework for electron microscopy — culminating in a preprint on arXiv.

Long term, I'm applying to doctoral programs in applied physics and materials science with a computational emphasis. I want to build the tools that let researchers see what classical simulation cannot, and eventually mentor the next generation of researchers — especially those underrepresented in physics.
